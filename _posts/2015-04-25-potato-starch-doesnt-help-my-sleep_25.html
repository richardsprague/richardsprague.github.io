--- layout: post title: Potato starch doesn't help my sleep date: '2015-04-25T10:05:00.000-07:00' author: Richard Sprague tags: - QS - Biology modified_time: '2015-04-25T10:05:03.720-07:00' thumbnail: http://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-TDVQ565BjVQ/VTvHg8vJeDI/AAAAAAADBBI/oBdJ2U8iLwA/s72-c/Pasted_Image_4_25_15__9_52_AM.jpg?imgmax=800 blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-8203221.post-821356114413559133 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.richardsprague.com/2015/04/potato-starch-doesnt-help-my-sleep_25.html --- I've been experimenting with the relationship between sleep and resistant starch, taking a few tablespoons of Bob's Red Mill potato starch, which some people think improves sleep by feeding the helpful Bifidobacterium that may play a role in producing up to 80% of the body's serotonin.

There’s no question the potato starch raised my Bifido levels:


But it seems not to have changed my sleep, either in overall hours:

or in a more precise measure, like REM:

You can see from these charts that I did notice some fantastic nights of sleep after starting potato starch, but there were plenty of other nights when my sleep was back to normal and sometimes worse. If I hadn’t measured so carefully, I’d be tempted to overplay the good and underplay the not-so-good. If there’s a psychosomatic effect, then yeah it may have made me feel better, but in terms of actual sleep time, resistant starch doesn’t seem to have helped.
