--- layout: post title: Finger length predicts academic aptitude date: '2004-10-29T22:57:00.000-07:00' author: Richard Sprague tags: modified_time: '2004-10-29T22:57:18.896-07:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-8203221.post-109911583889813203 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.richardsprague.com/2004/10/finger-length-predicts-academic.html --- A report from Mark Brosnan, the lead author from the University of Bath, UK, whose work has been submitted to the British Journal of Psychology and reported in New Scientist says that men have slightly shorter index fingers than ring fingers, while with women the two are the same length. Finger length is known to be associated with the level of hormone exposure in the womb.

Interestingly, however, a study of academics showed that people with the "female" pattern tended to gravitate to math and physics, while those with the "male" pattern seemed attracted to social science departments.
