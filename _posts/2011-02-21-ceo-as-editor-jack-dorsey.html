--- layout: post title: CEO as Editor (Jack Dorsey) date: '2011-02-21T23:23:00.000-08:00' author: Richard Sprague tags: - Podcast modified_time: '2011-02-21T23:23:00.175-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-8203221.post-9151362949777782135 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.richardsprague.com/2011/02/ceo-as-editor-jack-dorsey.html --- I often listen to Stanford’s Entrepreneurial Thought Leaders podcast, but there's a particularly good one this month with Jack Dorsey.  Best known as the co-founder of Twitter, he’s a wonderful speaker: short, clear, to-the-point, with great takeaways like:
“Make every detail perfect, and limit the number of details.”
“Expect the unexpected; and wherever possible, be the unexpected.”
He wraps this in the idea of “CEO as editor”, how leaders should be responsible for the story of their organization: hiring (the cast), internal and external communication, and attracting customers and investors. Apple, he reminds us, is one of the truly great story-telling organizations: their business cycle revolves around events and unveilings.
His talk is about 30 minutes: perfect for a commute.
