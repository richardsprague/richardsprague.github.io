Responsible overviews of the Quantified Self movement usually include an obligatory warning about the privacy dangers implications.  “The technology has intriguing potential to help people, but what about privacy?”

Self-tracking requires, by definition, self-awareness. You won’t track yourself – you can’t track yourself – without at least some basic interest in the data. Completely passive self-tracking is an oxymoron: if you don’t view the data or take some action based on the results, then it’s not self-tracking – it’s spying.
