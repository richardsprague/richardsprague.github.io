No wonder so many people are concerned about sustainability on Mercer Island.  Look at this fact:  Mercer Island has 3,452 people per square mile.  That means we pack more people into less space than the following cities:

City People per square mile
New York 2,050
San Francisco 2,350
Los Angeles 2,750
Kuala Lampur (Malaysia) 2,750
Beirut (Lebanon) 2,800
Nagoya (Japan) 3,150

I am not making this up.  Check the stats for yourself.

Everyone knows that population growth is a serious problem, right?  Species depletion, irreversible habitat loss, global warming--the unchecked greed of corporations and "globalization" is destroying our planet!  Even at the local level, you see the problems creeping up on us right here on Mercer Island: homelessness, and our declining incomes relative to the rest of the country.  To reverse this we all need to do our part, right?

I have a different theory.  People who think the world is overpopulated are really saying it's overcrowded with the wrong kind of people.  I don't care what the anti-population people think;  Mercer Island is a wonderful place full of great natural beauty and resources that is mostly thanks to all the people who live here.  Yes, we have a dense population

 

My favorite example is The Netherlands, one of the most densely-populated countries on earth.  At 395 sq/km, it is more crowded than Japan (339), India (336), or China (138), yet it the world's 3rd largest exporter of food (only the U.S. and France export more).
